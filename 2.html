Solution 2: Improving Access to Nitrogen Fertilizers

Analysis of our data

Access to fertilizers is a very important part of improving food security around the world. Fertilizers allow farmers to use their soils more efficiently and produce higher yields, without needing to take up more land or engineer new breeds of crops.

We evaluated the use of fertilizers containing nitrogen (N), phosphate (P₂O₅), and potash (K₂O), and pesticides in comparison to the percentage of people experiencing moderate or severe food insecurity. Here are scatterplots of these relationships:

After performing a Pearson Correlation Test, we found the following p-values:

Nitrogen: 4.112757737568049e-10
Phosphate: 1.958639597457427e-05
Potash: 3.001307226025263e-07
Pesticides: 0.0037585407775500607

As we can see, the use of nitrogen fertilizer is most strongly correlated with a reduction in the prevalence of food insecurity.

In addition, a study conducted by Wei et al. stated, “The dry matter was maximized and saturated between N300 and N450 (Fig. 4a), suggesting that N fertilizer is always necessary for garlic growth and that the conventional N rate of 300 kg ha-1 is appropriate for maximized growth.”


From this, nitrogen-based fertilizers tend to be more influential to plants than their phosphorus and potash counterparts, which could have a positive effect on food production, and therefore caloric intake.

The following two graphs show, on the left, the countries which use nitrogen fertilizer, and on the right, the prevalence of food insecurity in various countries.

As you can see, using nitrogen fertilizer has a quite significant effect on the prevalence of food insecurity in many nations.

distribute subsidized nitrogen fertilizer to farmers in developing countries.
