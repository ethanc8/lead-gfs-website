Implementation of Blockchain in Supply Chains

Some countries may have tampered with their data or not provided all of their data, so it wasn’t easy to clearly analyze all of the data provided.

To solve this, we propose blockchain technology that can be used to increase the traceability of records within the food supply chain.

This will enable further transparency for calorie intake and production records, enabling proper analysis of the supply chain.

Stated in a study by Akram et al., “Documenting each transaction on the blockchain makes it possible to give stakeholders an accurate picture of a product's whereabouts, status, and conditions.”

Blockchain can serve as a great way for users alike to see how their food gets to the table, and for reporting incidents revolving around the supply chain.

Furthermore, "Blockchain allows them to check and certify suppliers and ensure their adherence to food safety and quality requirements."(Akram et al.)

Not only is blockchain technology useful for better monitoring and traceability, it can also check for certification and food quality in the supply chain.

Gathering data will no longer be hard to find, or shallow and unspecfic. With blockchain, all the data will be in front of us, enabling better planning and stronger food security
