About


Global food security is an issue that results from numerous places, factors, and situations. These can range from global warming, to conflict between countries. The main root of food security problems, however, is the supply chain. The food supply chain accounts for all producing, processing, and distributing of different food sources throughout the world. With a fundamental understanding of how the supply chain operates, finding inconsistencies or drops in production would be crucial to coming up with countermeasures. To try and tackle the UN sustainability goal of “Zero Hunger”, this study will aim to conduct an analysis of food supply chain data to look for patterns. As these patterns are found, they will be interpreted to see if they represent an active problem in the supply chain. This will help us create some solutions to specific ongoing supply issues that are happening, enabling stronger food security. Coming up with solutions to this problem gives people better access to food, which can help fix or alleviate other problems revolving around food scarcity. This, in turn, can lead to the improvement of other sustainability goals. Not all supply chain issues are going to be addressed in this study, but the ones proposed could be an essential step in the right direction for fixing hunger problems around the world.
